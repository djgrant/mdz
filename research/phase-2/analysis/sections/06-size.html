Research question 2: where does size break execution?

Corpus-wide:The phase-1 slip at 200 statements did not recur

Phase 2 ran no dedicated size experiment; every record carries size metadata, and the size question was read off the whole corpus. Two observations came out of it.

First, the phase-1 slip did not reproduce. Phase 1 measured step accuracy slipping from ~0.99 at 100 statements to 0.92 at 200. E3's internal arm re-ran that setup and haiku scored 1.0 on assigns and emits in all eight runs, at both sizes. The sample is small – two seeds, one model.

Second, the runs that went wrong – SPAWN calls sent to the wrong tool (§2), payloads the workers refused as an attack (§2), assignments never written to the store (§3), the one clean program wrongly halted (§1) – all went wrong on programs well inside the executable range. The brief's concern that tool-heavy statements would drag the ceiling down found no support: E2's agentic programs were statement-light and tool-heavy, and their skeletons executed at ceiling.
