MDZ research · phase 2

Accountable execution

Phase 1 showed that models can execute MDZ programs. Phase 2 tested whether that execution can be verified from the outside: procedures shaped like real tasks, run by the model acting on its own, with every trace captured by the harness – the test framework that ran the programs and recorded the traces – rather than reported by the model.

E1 · strict mode 10/10 wrong types caught

Programs carried one planted fault, and models were told to validate before executing. They halted on every wrong type annotation. Broken syntax got silently repaired in 9 runs of 10.

 E2 · map-reduce skill 24/24 payloads intact

Programs fanned a list of items out to subagents. Every item and its instruction arrived unchanged. The only failures: two runs put the work on a todo list instead of spawning agents, and one batch of workers declined to carry out an instruction, treating it as a possible prompt injection – adversarial instructions smuggled in through the data.

 E3 · state store ¼ of writes logged

Models were told to save every variable update to an external store as they executed. They computed the values correctly but logged only a quarter of them, while final outputs stayed near-perfect.

 E4 · procedure vs goal 18/20 vs 7/20 compliant

Ten tasks where written policy demands a harsher outcome than a helpful assistant's instinct – for example, denying a refund requested on day 16 and offering no goodwill credit.

Policy written as a step-by-step procedure: followed 18/20. The same policy compressed to a one-line goal: 7/20, with models producing the helpful outcome the policy forbids.
