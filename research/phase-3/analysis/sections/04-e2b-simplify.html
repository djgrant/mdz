Does diverge-then-select beat a single context? Subjective selection

Experiment 2b:The judge agrees with the benchmark — ralph wins here too

E2b swaps the benchmark for taste. Targets are over-abstracted modules (~300–400 lines, passing test suites) whose abstraction debt is layered — indirection wrapping duplication wrapping speculative generality — so three successive rounds of genuine simplification are each productive. /simplify is the compound MDZ skill: three directional heuristics fan out per iteration, an inner reduce picks the winner, the winner seeds the next iteration, and a spawned fresh-context judge picks the best iteration at the end. /simplify-goal holds the same heuristics and plan in prose; /simplify-ralph is the same heuristics as a single-pass instruction run three times, fresh sessions, last pass ships unjudged. 2 targets × 3 variants × 2 models × n=3 = 36 runs.

Outcome is scored by a pairwise sonnet judge — order counterbalanced, both candidates presented with the original — across the three arm pairings per target/model/rep: 36 verdicts. All 36 runs passed the test gate, so the judge is the discriminator.

The verdicts are one-directional: ralph beats the skill 9–3, beats the goal 9–3, and the goal edges the skill 7–5. Pooled win rates: ralph 0.75, goal 0.42, skill 0.33. The subjective selector reached the same conclusion as E2a's benchmark — and the skill's diverge-and-select machinery came last, behind even the prose restatement of its own plan. The judge's reasons are consistent in tenor: ralph's output had actually collapsed the machinery into plain code, where skill-arm output too often shipped conservative or near-unchanged files.

The mechanism trace shows the skill arm executing its shape unevenly, and along a model split. Haiku mostly ran the notation faithfully — full 3-per-iteration fan-out in 5 of 6 runs (four runs even ran a fourth iteration), and it spawned the final judge in 4 of 6. Sonnet never once finished the shape: all six sonnet skill runs fanned out the first iteration (3 spawns) then abandoned the loop and the judge — spawn pattern [3, 0, 0], judge-spawn rate 0. The stronger model overrode the procedure it was given; the weaker model followed it. Delegation fidelity, phase 2's headline mechanism, inverted by capability. The goal arm spawned nothing at all, as in E2a. Winner-index — which iteration the skill's judge picked — was recoverable from only 1 of 36 records, so the winners-mid-sequence question stays unanswered.

Verdict: subjective selection replicates the objective result. Three fresh passes with no kept alternatives beat both orchestrated variants under a counterbalanced judge (win rate 0.75 vs 0.33 for the skill), and the test gate never once rescued the skill's selection step, because nothing failed it. The compound skill's own shape was only executed in full by the cheaper model; the capable model quietly reduced it to a single fan-out and shipped. Same budget caveat as E2a: ralph spends 2–5× the tokens.
