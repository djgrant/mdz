Does diverge-then-select beat a single context? Objective selection

Experiment 2a:The ralph loop routs both orchestrated variants

A slow-but-correct target program with a test suite and a benchmark, built with headroom in several directions (algorithm, caching, data structure, I/O batching). Three content-matched variants, invoked identically, the strategy strings verbatim across all three:

  • /optimise — the MDZ consumer skill driving map-reduce.mdz: five strategy hints, one worker per strategy, the reduce runs tests and benchmark on every candidate and ships the fastest that passes;
  • /optimise-goal — the same five hints and the same explore-then-select plan as prose, free to fan out with subagents;
  • /optimise-ralph — the same five hints and the goal alone, run as three fresh sequential sessions in the same sandbox. No candidates, no selection; re-running is the plan.

The headline metric is the speedup of the shipped solution — baseline benchmark time over shipped benchmark time, both re-run by the harness; the model's own numbers are not trusted. 2 targets × 3 variants × 2 models × n=3 = 36 runs. Every one of the 36 shipped a solution that passes the tests, so speedup is the whole comparison.

110.8× / 81.1×ralph median speedup (ledger / logscan)
1.8× / 3.9×MDZ skill median speedup
1.3× / 2.7×goal-prompt median speedup

The result is not close. Ralph's medians are 110.8× on ledger and 81.1× on logscan; the MDZ skill manages 1.8× and 3.9×; the prose goal 1.3× and 2.7×. The mechanism trace explains the shape. The skill did what its notation says — a mean of 5.4 worker spawns per run, one per strategy — but each worker gets one shot, and the reduce can only pick the best single-shot candidate. The goal variant, free to fan out, essentially never did (0.08 spawns per run): it tinkered sequentially inside one context and shipped the least. Ralph's three fresh sessions each started from the previous pass's improved program, and compounding three passes of "make it faster" beat one round of best-of-five.

Within the orchestrated pair, the ordering is consistent: the MDZ skill sits above the goal prompt in every cell (medians 1.8× vs 1.3× and 3.9× vs 2.7×). The notation did buy real fan-out where the prose plan produced none — the phase-2 delegation-fidelity finding again — it is just that fan-out was the wrong mechanism for this task.

Budget caveat. A ralph run is three full sessions against the single session of the other arms; its token budget ran 2–5× the single-session variants. Some of the gap is compute, not shape. But the goal variant had the same subagent budget available as the skill and declined to spend it, and the skill spent its budget on parallel single-shot candidates rather than sequential compounding — the shape finding survives the budget asymmetry even if the magnitude does not.

Verdict: on objective selection the tie-breaker question never arose — nothing tied. Iteration with fresh context beat best-of-N selection outright (medians ~102× vs 3.0× vs 1.7× pooled). The MDZ skill's fan-out is faithful and consistently beats the content-matched prose plan, but the map-reduce shape itself is the wrong tool here: keeping alternatives paid less than compounding passes.
