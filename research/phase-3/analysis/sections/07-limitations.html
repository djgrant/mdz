Limitations

  • Ralph's token budget is 2–5× the single-session arms. A ralph run is three fresh sessions; the other arms get one. Both E2 verdicts are therefore about shape-plus-budget, not shape alone. The mechanism reading survives (the goal arm declined a budget it had; the skill spent its budget on parallel single shots), but "ralph wins at matched spend" is unproven.
  • Benchmark noise from concurrent load was eliminated by rescoring. E2a's original benchmark numbers were taken while the machine was running other experiment load; all shipped candidates were re-benchmarked on a quiet machine, and those rescored times are what e2a-scores.jsonl and this report use. Baselines were re-measured in the same pass, so speedups are internally consistent.
  • E2b's skill arm does not test its own design. Two confounds, found post hoc. Sonnet never executed the program (0/6 complete pipelines), so half the skill-arm verdicts judge an improvisation. And the program relays code by value through worker prompts, so even compliant runs measure text-relay drift as much as diverge-then-select. Both were visible in the mechanism data at scoring time and should have gated the arm comparison before any win rates were reported. Both confounds are fixed in the second run set, which is itself smoke-scale (n=1 per cell) — mechanism evidence, not effect sizes.
  • E2b's winner index was rarely recoverable. The skill asks its spawned judge to pick the best iteration; that pick could be recovered from only 1 of 36 records, so the winner-index distribution — whether winners sit mid-sequence — remains unmeasured. Judge-spawn presence and per-iteration spawn counts are trustworthy; what the judge chose is not.
  • Session-limit interruptions required resumes. Several long runs (E3's 800-statement cells especially, plus a handful of E1 cells) hit provider session limits and were re-run by id; the results files carry the superseded errored records, and scoring deduplicates by id with the last record winning. No cell lost its n, but the affected runs' wall-clock and token figures describe the successful attempt only.
  • E1's evidence-level finding rests on sonnet's ceiling. With the full log, sonnet is perfect in both arms — which shows the log substitutes for position, but also means the log condition has no headroom to detect a form effect. A harder task family could yet find one there.
  • The internal arm's assign trace is still self-reported. E3's internal assign accuracy (0.769 at 400, 0.339 at 800) inherits the phase-1 confound: a formatting lapse is indistinguishable from an execution error, and at 800 the truncated traces make the number mostly a completion measure. Only the store arm's set-log trace is mechanically captured. Emit accuracy, the primary metric, is unaffected.
  • Cell sizes are small and coverage is narrow. Most cells are n=3–6; E3 is haiku-only; E2 uses two synthetic targets per sub-experiment, built to have optimisation headroom and layered abstraction debt respectively. The two-orders-of-magnitude E2a gap and the 800-statement E3 collapse survive the noise; the finer rates (E2b's 9–3 splits, haiku's cut-level E1 rates) are order-of-magnitude estimates. The E2b judge is a single sonnet configuration, counterbalanced but not replicated across judges.
